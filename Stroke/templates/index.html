Introduction

The objective of the Project is to develop a preliminary screening tool which can be used to identify the likelihood of an individual having a stroke based on general contributing factors.
Project consist of 3 main parts: stroke mortality rate analysis, leading causes of stroke analysis , data exploratory analysis and ML model building. Data from Kaggle was used as the basis for the predictive model.

Stroke Facts

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to a region of the brain is suddenly blocked or when a rupture occurs starving the brain cells of oxygen and nutrients. Blockage obstructing the flow of blood to a region of the brain is called an ischemic stroke and accounts for 87% of all strokes. The rupturing of blood vessel is called a hemorrhagic stroke and accounts for 13% of all strokes.

Charateristics of the Data Set

Predominate medical conditions that increase the chances of having a stroke are High Blood Pressure, Heart disease, Diabetes, Smoking and Obesity.

The term comorbidities means the simultaneous presence of two or more medical conditions and is a major factor in stroke risk.

A summary of the data trends are as follows:

  • 59% Female, 41% Male
  • 5% of dataset have had a stroke
  • 92% of the strokes occur over the age of 50
  • 38% of the strokes are in the age range 70 - 79
  • 86% of stroke victims had comorbidities
  • Obesity and Current/Former Smoker status are top 2 comorbidities of the various combinations

Data Set Exploratory & Model Building

Our process for data set aquistion through modeling and deployment are summarized as follows:

  • Dataset acquired from the Kaggle website.
  • Information was downloaded and imported into Python as a .CSV file.
  • Data clean-up was performed using imputation, Labeling, and One Hot coding
  • Stroke Class imbalance as checked using SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique).
  • Three Linear and Five Tree models were evaluated with our final selection being Logistice Regression due to having the best f1-score and recall scores.
  • A flask app was built to allow for the exchange of model inputs and to provide a return of model prediction results as a percent liklihood of Stroke.
  • We built a "Stroke Predictor" input form for our website visitors to enter their own profiles based on the features used in our analysis.
